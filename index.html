Find your happiness in Purulia.

Hundreds of thousands of tourists come to visit Purulia annually to witness the charm of the natural scenery of Ayodhya, Turga Falls, PPSP Upper and Lower Dam, Dawri khal in Kesto Bazar Dam, Lahoria Shiv Mandir, Matha and Kuilapal, falls and tribal habitations of Ajodhya Hills and Bagmundih, dams like Panchet, Murguma Dam and Futiari, heritage buildings like Panchakot Raj Place, trekking range of Matha proud with Pakhi Pahar, and Joychandi Pahar, picnic spots like Baranti, Duarsini, Doladanga, Jamuna, traditional folk dance and culture like Chhou Dance and Jhumur Song.

Purulia District

History

Purulia is one of the oldest places in West Bengal, dating back to the 5th century A.D. It was a part of the 16 Mahajanapadas, and was also part of the country known as Vajra-bhumi in ancient times.During the 10thâ€“13th century, a Jain settlement flourished in the western part of West Bengal, covering the present districts of Purulia, Bankura, and Jhargram. During this period, they built many brick and stone temples, following the Oriya Duel style architecture.

Culture

Purulia is a district in West Bengal with a rich cultural heritage. It has a mixed culture of Bengal, Jharkhand, and Odisha, and every cultural event has a tribal touch.Shiber Gajan It is one of the longest and most famous festivals held at Purulia from the middle of March to the end of May. You can, however, find other names for this festival. The local Santhals calls it Shiber Gajan while the other notable tribesmen like the Kurmi and Korhas name it as Chait Puja and Charak Puja.

Tourism

Purulia! A hidden gem in West Bengal, India. The district is known for its rich cultural heritage, stunning landscapes, and vibrant festivals. The Ajodhya Hills offer a breathtaking backdrop for nature enthusiasts, with lush greenery and trekking trails. The iconic Charida village is famous for its traditional Chhau masks and sculptures, showcasing the region's artistic flair. Don't miss the annual Chhau Dance Festival, where performers bring folklore to life through this vibrant dance form. Baranti, with its serene lake, is perfect for a peaceful retreat. And if you're into history, explore the ruins of the ancient temples at Banda Deul and Joychandi Pahar.

Gharpanchkot

It has the ruins of some forts built by the kings of Panchakot in 1600 A.D. Garh Panchkot is a ruined fort located in the eastern part of India at the foothills of Panchet Hill in the district of Purulia, West Bengal. The ruins of the Panchkot Palace are a silent testimony to the Bargi attack during the 18th century.
